The Weekly Commitment Analysis

To earn a roster spot at the college level, training doesn't start and end with your club team practices. Successful recruits treat soccer like a craft, dedicating an average of 15 to 22 hours per week to athletic development.

Reflection Questions

Question 1: The High School Commitment Check

Am I excited by the idea of dedicated daily individual training (1,000+ touches a day), or does this feel like a chore? What am I willing to deprioritize in my life to make room?

Question 2: The College Commitment Check

Do I love the game enough to make it the central pillar of my college experience, even if it means missing out on traditional "college social life" experiences?

Question 3: The Role Reality Check

Would I be happier sitting on the bench at a famous Division I school, or playing 90 minutes a game as an impactful leader at a highly competitive Division III or NAIA school?

Question 4: The "Broken Leg" Test

If I tore my ACL and soccer was taken away from me entirely, would I still be genuinely happy attending this college for its campus, academics, and student body?

Please write at least a few words for each question above!
